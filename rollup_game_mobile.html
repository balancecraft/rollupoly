Fund Prospectus & Rules of Engagement
The Objective
🏆
Be the last fund standing. Acquire companies, extract fees, and drive your competitors into bankruptcy. The GP always wins — except when they don't.
On Your Turn
🎲
Roll the dice. Your token hops space-by-space to your new position. Long moves snap directly — we have a fund to run.
🏢
Land on an unowned company? Execute an LBO — pay 40% equity upfront. The remaining 60% is loaded as leveraged debt onto your fund.
⚡
Pass or can't afford it? Early game: property stays on market — no auction until the first sector monopoly is completed. Once rollup activity heats up, passing triggers a forced auction opening at 50% of list. All players bid.
💸
Land on a rival's company? Pay their service fee. Sector rollup (2+ companies): 1.5×. Full sector monopoly: 6×.
⇄
Propose a deal any time on the active turn. Offer and request any combination of cash and companies. The other player accepts or rejects.
The Board
💰
Close the Fund (Start): Collect $200M management fee every lap. Regardless of performance. That's the model.
🅿️
Free Parking: Collect $100M in carried interest. Taxed at 20% — lower than your housekeeper's rate. For now.
🏝️
Tax Haven: Route through the Caymans. Collect $50M. Perfectly legal.
💸
Placement Fee space: Pay $150M to the bank. The placement agent who raised your fund gets paid regardless of performance. Non-negotiable.
📋
Term Sheet / LP Capital Call: Draw a card. Market events, legal consequences, or fund-level shocks. Some affect all players.
Warren Hearing
⚖️
Go to Warren Hearing: Sent directly to the subcommittee corner. Miss 1 turn. You are under oath.
🔨
Warren Hearing corner: Split like Monopoly jail — Just Visiting (passed through, no penalty) or In the Hearing (sent here, frozen). Land here normally and you're fine.
🚔
Return visit = Criminal conviction. The committee refers you to the DOJ. The DOJ wins. Miss 3 turns in a privatized facility — and pay extortionate fees each frozen turn just to use the phone. The corner shows bars.
📋
Criminal record: Once convicted, you pay a +50% surcharge on all rent for the rest of the game. Shown as a line item when you pay.
Debt & Interest
📉
Every LBO loads 60% of the purchase price as debt. Each time you pass Close the Fund, you pay 10% interest on total outstanding debt — after collecting your management fee.
🏦
Extend & Pretend: If interest would bankrupt you, choose to defer it — but it doubles next lap. Shown on your player card. The can doesn't disappear. It just gets heavier.
Rollups & Monopolies
🔗
Own 2+ companies in a sector: fees rise to 1.5×. Own the entire sector: fees jump to 6×. This is the rollup strategy. This is the whole game.
⚠️
Community Impact: Completing a full sector monopoly triggers a card describing what happened to the people inside it. No mechanical effect. Just so you know.
📊
The board center tracks harm in real time — facilities understaffed, renters displaced, journalists eliminated — as properties are acquired.
Antitrust & Legal Cards
⚖️
Clayton Act §7: Acquisition that substantially lessens competition. Your most recently acquired company in the most concentrated sector is divested. You keep the management fees.
⚖️
Clayton Act §8: Interlocking directorates between competing firms are prohibited. Fine.
📋
HSR Violation: Failed to notify the FTC and DOJ before closing. Miss 1 turn, pay fine.
📋
Tunney Act: Improper lobbying on a consent decree. Public hearings. Miss 1 turn, pay fine.
📋
Sherman Act §1: No-poach agreements suppressed wages across your portfolio. Miss 1 turn, pay fine.
💀
Fraudulent conveyance: Dividend recap before bankruptcy. Court claws back 30% of your cash.
🏛️
CFIUS Review: Foreign LP triggers national security review. Miss 1 turn, pay compliance costs. You took the money.
🏦
Extend & Pretend (card): Lenders roll over your debt. Collect $30M — but deferred interest doubles next lap. Sometimes the lender decides.
Bankruptcy
💀
Cash goes negative: fund liquidated. All portfolio companies return to market. The GP walks away with nothing — except the management fees already collected.
